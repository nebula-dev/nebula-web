About Lapis Labs

Shannon Lucas, Co-Founder of Catalyst Constellations, has over twenty years' experience working with startups, launching her own ventures, and driving innovation into the world's largest companies. She is a passionate thought leader about creating sustainable organizations in every sense of the word; for people, planet and profit.

Shannon launched Catalyst Constellations based on the recognition that there is a unique group people driving the most profound change in the world - Catalysts. This community has special set of superpowers and challenges. Shannon and her partner create tools, resources, and research, along with physical and virtual spaces to connect and support a highly curated global community of Catalysts; so they can more sustainably drive positive impact around the world. The tribe consists of Catalysts from organizations such as Amazon, Apple, GE, Hitachi, and Steelcase.

In her previous roles, as EVP of Emerging Business at Ericsson, Senior Innovation Architect at Cisco's Hyperinnovation Living Lab and Director of Innovation at Vodafone Global Enterprise, Shannon empowered Fortune 500 businesses to stay agile, competitive and sustainable through the adoption of new technologies and organizational structures. She created global customer innovation programs supporting all three horizons of innovation; shifting enterprise customer relationships to collaborative innovation partnerships. She has co-created new products with leading innovation giants including Hitachi, Microsoft, GE, Amazon, Intel, Citibank, & Thomson Reuters.

Shannon is a global thought leader on how intrapreneurship drives successful corporate innovation programs. Shannon shared her vision on collaborative innovation at TedX. Armed with the unique leadership and team lessons of having served as a mountain rescue volunteer, this Bay Area disruptor developed her career with cutting edge technology experience at companies like Microsoft and T-Mobile. She started a successful technology consulting business, worked at several startups, and has served in various board capacities including The Barbara Bush Foundation, IoTC, CorStone and Bay Area Mountain Rescue.
